The Challenge

SaaS Punishes Unfocused Product Decisions Faster Than Any Other Market.

Subscription revenue means the market re-votes on your product every month. Weak prioritization shows up as churn. Fuzzy positioning shows up as stalled pipeline. And a reactive roadmap shows up as a competitor announcing the feature your customers asked you for first.

Roadmap ruled by requests

The backlog is a queue of customer asks and sales promises, not a strategy. Every sprint ships something; the product doesn't move anywhere.

Retention nobody owns

Churn gets discussed in every board meeting and owned in none. Nobody is doing the user research that explains why customers actually leave.

Positioning that blurs

The category is crowded, the message is generic, and prospects can't tell you apart from the other three tabs they have open.

Senior product gap

Engineers are strong, founders are stretched, and the senior product layer — the one that turns market data into conviction — doesn't exist yet.

Common Questions

What SaaS leaders ask before we get started.

What does a SaaS product consultant actually do?

In our case: define product strategy from real market research, run the roadmap and delivery cadence, and build the go-to-market engine — positioning, enablement, demand generation. The difference from most consultants is that we do it from inside your team, not from a slide deck.

How is this different from hiring an agency?

Agencies deliver recommendations and move on. We embed, own outcomes, and stay — the engagement is measured in shipped product and market traction, not deliverables handed over.

Do you only work with SaaS?

SaaS is a core focus, but we've operated across software, AI, hardware, media, and government technology. If it's a technology product with a real market to win, the model applies.
