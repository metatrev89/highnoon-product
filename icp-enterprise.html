The Challenge

The Bigger the Organization, the Harder It Is to See Clearly.

Enterprise teams are great at optimizing what's already there. What they often don't have is the independent perspective to figure out what should exist next — and someone who can actually lead the execution once you've decided to move. Internal politics, org complexity, and proximity to the existing product all make it hard to see the full picture clearly.

🔭

Innovation gaps

Large organizations move slowly, and internal teams are too close to existing products to identify transformational opportunities in adjacent markets before a more agile competitor gets there first.

🧪

Strategic product discovery

The organization needs rigorous market research and independent analysis to evaluate new product lines or platform expansions before committing significant capital — without internal bias shaping the conclusions.

🏛️

Complex GTM environments

Enterprise products require nuanced positioning, multi-stakeholder sales cycles, and enablement infrastructure that internal teams are rarely structured to build quickly alongside their existing operational responsibilities.

🔐

Regulated & mission-critical environments

In legal, compliance, and government technology environments, product leadership requires both deep technical understanding and the operational discipline to deliver under strict standards — a combination generalist firms cannot replicate.

Common Questions

What enterprise leaders usually ask me before we get started.

How do you support enterprise product strategy?

I come in as an independent senior product partner — I do the rigorous market research to identify new product opportunities at scale, I refine existing product lines where they need it, and I build enterprise-grade go-to-market strategies for complex multi-stakeholder environments. Every engagement is grounded in real data and accountable to actual business outcomes, not just deliverables.

What's the difference between you and a typical product consultant?

A generalist consultant gives you frameworks and a presentation and moves on. I deliver the strategy and stay to execute it — I own the outcomes, not just the slide deck. I've operated as a product executive across software, hardware, media, and government technology. That's practitioner experience, not analyst experience.

Can you work in government technology or regulated environments?

Yes — I have direct experience in DoD and government technology programs, including work with VivSoft Technologies, a DoD Priority 1 contractor. My background includes Agile, DevSecOps, and mission-critical product environments where compliance and delivery precision aren't optional. I know how to operate in those environments.

How do you work alongside a large internal product team?

I'm not coming in to replace anyone — I'm coming in to add the independent strategic layer that's hard to get from inside a large organization. The engagement gets scoped around the specific gap where an outside perspective creates the most leverage: new product discovery, GTM strategy for an upcoming launch, or a roadmap review that internal teams are too close to the existing product to do objectively.
