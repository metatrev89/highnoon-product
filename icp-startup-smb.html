The Challenge

You Can't Run a Company and a Product at the Same Time.

Founders are great at a lot of things — but running a company and leading product development at the same time usually means one of them suffers. Without someone dedicated to the product, roadmaps stall, teams go in different directions, and by the time you get to go-to-market there's no real strategy in place.

🧭

No dedicated product leader

The founding team is wearing every hat. Product strategy, stakeholder communication, and roadmap decisions all fall on the same one or two people already running the company.

🔍

Unclear product-market fit

The idea exists but the market validation, competitive landscape, and customer segmentation are undefined — making it impossible to build with confidence.

⏸

Stalled development

A product is partially built but lacks the structure, roadmap discipline, and team coordination to ship — burning runway without a clear path to launch.

📣

No go-to-market plan

The team can build — but doesn't know how to position, price, or sell. Launch momentum is lost without a clear GTM strategy in place before the product ships.

What High Noon Delivers

I Cover the Full Lifecycle — or Focus Where You Need Me Most.

I'm happy to come in and do all three — vision, build, and go-to-market — or focus on the specific piece where you need the most help right now. It's modular. Every engagement is scoped to your stage, your team, and what you're actually trying to accomplish.

  • 01
    Product Vision

    Before anyone builds anything, I do the market research to figure out where you can actually win. Competitive analysis, consumer segmentation, user research, economic trends — it all goes into a data-backed roadmap that tells your team exactly what to build and why. No guessing.

  • 02
    Product Management

    I come in fully embedded and run the product from Day 0 through Day 2. I lead the team, manage the roadmap, keep stakeholders aligned, and make sure nothing falls through the cracks. The goal is simple — products that actually ship, on time, without everything catching fire.

  • 03
    Go-to-Market Strategy

    Building the product is only half the job. I build the go-to-market strategy — positioning, messaging, sales enablement, demand generation — everything you need to actually execute and bring revenue in after launch day.

  • +
    Flexible Delivery Model

    I can embed alongside your existing team as a fractional product leader, or if you need someone to actually manage the development itself — design, engineering, the whole build — me and my team can handle that too. It's modular. We scope it to what you actually need.

Common Questions

What founders usually ask me before we get started.

What does High Noon actually do for a startup?

I come in as your senior product leader — I define the vision, lead the team through development, and build the go-to-market strategy to turn launch into real revenue. And it's modular — I can embed alongside your existing team, or if you need someone to actually manage the full build, me and my team can do that too.

When should a founder bring in a fractional CPO?

When you're managing product strategy on top of everything else you're already doing — or when a funding round creates pressure to move faster and you need a proven operator in the seat right now. A fractional engagement gives you senior expertise without the cost of a full-time executive hire.

Can you manage the actual product development, not just the strategy?

Yes. I can embed as your product leader if you already have developers, or me and my team can manage the full build — design, engineering coordination, launch — for founders who need to stand up a product function from scratch. Happy to do either.

What industries have you worked in?

Software, hardware, media, and government technology. I've led product initiatives across all of them, so if you're building a technology product I've likely worked in your space or something close to it.

How does an engagement get started?

We have a conversation first — I want to understand where you are, what you're building, and what you actually need help with. From there we scope it together — comprehensive across all three services or focused on the one area where we can create the most impact right now.
